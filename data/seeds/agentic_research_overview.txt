Title: Agentic research systems - overview

Summary:
This note sketches the lifecycle of an agentic research system.
Core stages:
- Intake: capture topic, constraints, and user intent.
- Memory: store documents and user interests for reuse.
- Retrieval: match topic and interests to relevant sources.
- Planning: build a research plan with scope, questions, and gaps.
- Refinement: iterate plan until it is ready for execution.
- Outline: produce a structured outline for manuscripts or reports.

Important considerations:
- Keep the system auditable: each skill should be explicit and inspectable.
- Avoid hidden heuristics; log decisions in the plan.
- Separate planning from execution to preserve user control.

Open questions:
- How to define readiness criteria for the plan?
- How much retrieval is enough before writing?
- What parts of the plan should be human-verified?
